Spaghetti

A Yummy and Delicious Spaghetti

Spaghetti is a long, thin, solid, cylindrical pasta made from durum wheat, water, and sometimes eggs. The name comes from the Italian word spaghetto, meaning "little string". It is a staple of Italian cuisine and is often served with sauces like tomato-based, meat, or vegetable sauces.

Ingredients

  • Spaghetti noodles
  • Ground beef
  • Olive oil
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Tomato sauce
  • Tomato paste
  • Italian seasoning
  • Dried basil
  • Sugar
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Fresh parsley or basil (for garnish)
  • Water (for boiling)

Steps

  1. Fill a large pot with water, add a generous amount of salt, and bring it to a rolling boil.
  2. Add the spaghetti noodles to the boiling water and stir to prevent them from sticking together.
  3. Cook the spaghetti according to the package directions until it reaches your desired tenderness, or al dente, which is still firm to the bite.
  4. While the pasta cooks, heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  5. Add the chopped onion and saut√© until it becomes soft and translucent, then add the minced garlic and cook for another minute.
  6. Add the ground beef to the skillet, breaking it up with a spoon, and cook until it is fully browned.
  7. Pour in the tomato sauce and tomato paste, add the Italian seasoning, basil, sugar, salt, and pepper, and stir to combine.
  8. Bring the sauce to a simmer and let it cook for at least 15-20 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  9. Before draining the pasta, reserve about a cup of the starchy pasta water.
  10. Drain the cooked spaghetti using a colander.
  11. Add the drained spaghetti directly to the skillet with the sauce and toss to combine, adding a splash of the reserved pasta water if needed to loosen the sauce.
  12. Serve the spaghetti hot, topped with grated Parmesan cheese and fresh parsley or basil.
